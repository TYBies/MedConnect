About Us

Our Story

Germany's healthcare system urgently needs more nurses. Meanwhile, Africa is home to countless qualified healthcare professionals who often face barriers — language, recognition, and relocation — when trying to advance their careers abroad.

Why We Started

This business was founded to bridge that gap: To help African healthcare workers achieve their dream of working in Europe, to guide them through the process of learning German, getting their qualifications recognized, and securing stable, well-paying jobs.

We support German healthcare institutions that urgently need skilled, dedicated professionals. In short: We connect talent with opportunity, and in doing so, we create hope, careers, and better healthcare for everyone.

Our Mission & Vision

Mission: Empowering nurses from Africa by guiding them through language training, credential recognition, job placement, and relocation support — while helping German healthcare institutions meet staffing needs with qualified, motivated professionals.

Vision: To be the most trusted recruitment bridge connecting African healthcare professionals with career opportunities in Germany.

Candidate Requirements

What you need to work as a nurse in Germany

  1. Language Requirement:
    Minimum B1 German proficiency. Accepted certificates: Goethe-Institut, telc, ÖSD, TestDaF.
  2. Education:
    Completed nursing diploma or degree from an accredited institution. Minimum of 3 years of nursing education (recognized in home country).
  3. Professional Requirements:
    Registered as a nurse in your home country. Proof of work experience (recommended: at least 1–2 years).
  4. Recognition in Germany (Anerkennung):
    Apply for recognition of foreign qualifications. May require adaptation training or a knowledge test.
  5. Other Documents:
    Valid passport, Police clearance certificate, Medical fitness certificate.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the entire process take? +

The complete process typically takes between 9–18 months, depending on your current German language level, the recognition process, and visa processing times.

Who pays for language training? +

Candidates are responsible for their language training costs. However, we provide guidance on finding affordable and accredited language programs.

Can I bring my family to Germany? +

Yes, once you have a valid work contract and visa, you can apply for family reunification to bring your spouse and children to Germany.

What support do you provide to employers? +

We offer candidate screening & qualification checks, support with documentation & recognition process, and full relocation assistance for healthcare institutions.
